AISSEE 2027: The Complete Guide to All India Sainik School Entrance Exam

The All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE) 2027 is the national-level entrance examination conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to Class 6 and Class 9 in all 33 Sainik Schools across India. AISSEE is held once a year, typically in January, and is the sole gateway for admission into the Sainik School system. Approximately 4 lakh students appear for AISSEE each year, competing for a limited number of seats across India.

Key Takeaway: AISSEE 2027 is expected to be held in January 2027. Applications will open around October–November 2026 on the NTA portal. The exam is OMR-based and tests Mathematics, General Knowledge, Language (English/Hindi), and Intelligence/Reasoning. Start your preparation at least 6 months in advance for the best results.

What Is AISSEE?

The All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE) is a nationally competitive entrance examination conducted annually by the National Testing Agency (NTA) on behalf of the Sainik Schools Society under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India.

The purpose of AISSEE is to select students for admission to Class 6 and Class 9 in the 33 Sainik Schools located across India. Sainik Schools are residential schools that follow the CBSE curriculum and are designed to prepare students — both academically and physically — for careers in the Indian Armed Forces, particularly through the National Defence Academy (NDA) and Indian Naval Academy (INA).

Syllabus Breakdown

Class 6 Syllabus

Mathematics

The Mathematics syllabus for AISSEE Class 6 is based on the Class 5 CBSE/NCERT curriculum:

  • Number System: Place value, comparing numbers, rounding off, Roman numerals
  • Arithmetic Operations: Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division of large numbers
  • Fractions & Decimals: Types of fractions, equivalent fractions, operations on fractions and decimals
  • Factors & Multiples: HCF, LCM, prime and composite numbers
  • Geometry: Basic shapes, angles, lines, triangles, circles, perimeter and area
  • Measurement: Length, weight, capacity, time, temperature
  • Data Handling: Bar graphs, pictographs, tables
  • Patterns: Number patterns, shape patterns
  • Profit & Loss: Basic concepts, simple problems
  • Simple Interest: Basic formula and calculations

General Knowledge (Science & Social Studies)

  • Science: Human body, plants, animals, food and nutrition, matter and materials, force and energy, natural phenomena
  • Social Studies: Indian history (ancient and medieval basics), geography of India, Indian constitution basics, famous personalities

English Language

  • Grammar: Parts of speech (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs), tenses, articles, prepositions, conjunctions
  • Vocabulary: Synonyms, antonyms, one-word substitutions, idioms and phrases
  • Comprehension: Reading passages with questions
  • Sentence Structure: Sentence formation, correction, transformation
  • Writing: Paragraph writing, letter writing basics

Intelligence / Reasoning

  • Verbal Reasoning: Series completion, analogy, classification, coding-decoding
  • Non-Verbal Reasoning: Pattern recognition, figure series, mirror images, water images
  • Logical Reasoning: Direction sense, blood relations, ranking, Venn diagrams
  • Mathematical Reasoning: Number puzzles, odd one out

Class 9 Syllabus

Mathematics

Based on the Class 8 CBSE/NCERT curriculum:

  • Number System: Rational numbers, exponents, squares and square roots, cubes and cube roots
  • Algebra: Linear equations in one variable, factorization, algebraic expressions and identities
  • Ratio & Proportion: Direct and inverse proportion, percentages, profit and loss, discount
  • Geometry: Quadrilaterals, polygons, construction of triangles, understanding quadrilaterals
  • Mensuration: Area of trapezium, polygon, surface area and volume of cube, cuboid, cylinder
  • Data Handling: Bar graphs, pie charts, probability basics
  • Introduction to Graphs: Cartesian plane, plotting points, linear graphs

English

  • Grammar: Tenses (all forms), voice (active and passive), narration (direct and indirect), subject-verb agreement
  • Vocabulary: Advanced synonyms, antonyms, word formation, contextual meaning
  • Comprehension: Advanced reading passages with inference-based questions
  • Writing Skills: Essay writing, letter writing (formal and informal), notice writing, message writing
  • Literature: Prose and poetry comprehension from NCERT textbooks

General Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)

  • Physics: Force and pressure, friction, sound, chemical effects of electric current, light
  • Chemistry: Materials (metals and non-metals), synthetic fibres and plastics, coal and petroleum, combustion and flame
  • Biology: Cell structure, micro-organisms, conservation of plants and animals, reproduction in animals

Social Studies

  • History: Indian history from 1700s to 1947 — British rule, freedom struggle, important movements and leaders
  • Geography: Resources and development, agriculture, industries, human resources, natural vegetation
  • Civics: Indian Constitution, fundamental rights and duties, parliament, judiciary, secularism

Intelligence / Reasoning

  • Verbal Reasoning: Analogies, classification, series, coding-decoding, logical sequence of words
  • Non-Verbal Reasoning: Figure matrix, pattern completion, paper folding and cutting, embedded figures
  • Logical Reasoning: Syllogisms, statement and conclusion, blood relations, direction sense, ranking and ordering

Application Process: Step by Step

The AISSEE application process is entirely online through the NTA portal. Here is a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Visit the Official Portal

Go to exams.nta.ac.in/AISSEE when the application window opens (expected October–November 2026).

Step 2: Register as a New User

  • Click on “New Registration” or “Apply for AISSEE 2027”
  • Enter basic details: name, date of birth, email address, mobile number
  • Create a password and note down the Application Number

Step 3: Fill the Application Form

Log in with your Application Number and password. Fill in:

  • Personal Details: Name, father’s name, mother’s name, date of birth, gender, category
  • Contact Details: Address, state, district, pin code
  • Educational Details: Current class, school name, board
  • Choice of School: Select preferred Sainik Schools (up to 5 preferences typically)
  • Exam Centre: Choose your preferred exam city

Step 4: Upload Documents

Upload scanned copies of:

  • Photograph — Passport-size, recent, JPEG format (10KB–200KB)
  • Signature — On white paper, JPEG format (4KB–30KB)
  • Category Certificate (if applicable)
  • Date of Birth Certificate

Step 5: Pay Application Fee

CategoryFee (Approx.)
General / OBC₹550 – ₹650
SC / ST₹400 – ₹450

Payment can be made via credit card, debit card, net banking, or UPI.

Step 6: Submit and Download Confirmation

  • Review all details carefully before final submission
  • Submit the form
  • Download and print the confirmation page for your records

Step 7: Download Admit Card

  • Admit cards are released approximately 1–2 weeks before the exam
  • Download from the NTA portal using your Application Number and Date of Birth
  • Print the admit card and carry it to the exam centre along with a valid photo ID

Important: Keep your Application Number and password safe. You will need them throughout the admission process — for admit card download, answer key objection, result checking, and counselling.

Study Tips for Book Usage

  1. Start with NCERT — Always complete NCERT textbooks first before moving to reference books
  2. Practice, don’t just read — Solve exercises and problems actively
  3. Previous year papers are gold — They give you the real feel of the exam
  4. Don’t buy too many books — 3–4 well-chosen books are better than 10 half-read ones
  5. Use books for different purposes — NCERT for concepts, guide books for practice, solved papers for exam simulation

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is AISSEE and who conducts it?

AISSEE stands for All India Sainik School Entrance Exam. It is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) on behalf of the Sainik Schools Society under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India. The exam is held once a year for admission to Class 6 and Class 9 in 33 Sainik Schools across India.

2. When will AISSEE 2027 be held?

AISSEE 2027 is expected to be held in January 2027 (typically the 2nd or 3rd week). The application form is expected to be available from October–November 2026. Official dates will be announced on exams.nta.ac.in/AISSEE.

3. What is the eligibility for AISSEE 2027?

For Class 6, candidates must be aged 10–12 years as on 31 March 2027 and studying in Class 5. For Class 9, candidates must be aged 13–15 years as on 31 March 2027 and studying in Class 8. Both categories require Indian citizenship. Girls can apply for Class 6 admission.

4. Is there negative marking in AISSEE?

No. There is no negative marking in AISSEE for either Class 6 or Class 9. Students should attempt all questions, even if unsure of the answer, as there is no penalty for incorrect responses.

5. How many questions are there in AISSEE?

For Class 6, there are 125 questions worth 300 marks. For Class 9, there are 150 questions worth 400 marks. All questions are multiple-choice (MCQ) format.

6. Can girls apply for AISSEE?

Yes. Girls can apply for Class 6 admission through AISSEE. The Government of India approved the admission of girls to Sainik Schools starting from the 2021–22 academic session. However, Class 9 admission for girls is not yet available in most Sainik Schools.

7. What is the fee of Sainik School?

The annual fee for Sainik Schools ranges from approximately ₹50,000 to ₹1,50,000 depending on the school and category. SC/ST candidates receive significant fee concessions. Defence wards also receive fee benefits. Scholarships are available for meritorious students.

8. How should I prepare for AISSEE?

Start preparation at least 6 months before the exam. Focus on:

  1. Complete NCERT textbooks for the relevant class
  2. Solve previous year AISSEE papers (at least 5 years)
  3. Practice reasoning and mental ability daily
  4. Build general knowledge through current affairs and NCERT Science/SST
  5. Take weekly mock tests in the last 2 months
  6. See our detailed 6-month preparation plan above

9. What is the cut-off for AISSEE?

Cut-offs vary by school, category, and year. As a general guideline, for Class 6, General category candidates typically need 210–240 out of 300 (70–80%) to qualify for top schools. For Class 9, the range is approximately 280–320 out of 400 (70–80%). SC/ST cut-offs are significantly lower. See our detailed cut-off analysis above.

10. How many Sainik Schools are there in India?

There are currently 33 Sainik Schools in India, spread across 26 states and union territories. The first Sainik Schools were established in 1961, and the newest ones (Amethi, Chandrapur, Jhansi, Mainpuri) were added between 2019 and 2020.
