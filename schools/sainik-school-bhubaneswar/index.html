Sainik School Bhubaneswar – Odisha's Premier NDA School

Sainik School Guide Team • Jul 9, 2026 • 8 min read

    Key Takeaway: Sainik School Bhubaneswar was started on 15 January 1962 by the late Shri Biju Patnaik, then Chief Minister of Odisha, and was inaugurated at its permanent campus by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on 8 January 1964. Located in Bhubaneswar, the capital city of Odisha, the school is affiliated with the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) and operates as a co-educational residential school under the Sainik Schools Society, Ministry of Defence, Government of India. The school prepares cadets for entry into the National Defence Academy (NDA) and other defence institutions.

    History and Foundation

    Founded by Biju Patnaik

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar owes its existence to the vision of Late Shri Biju Patnaik, the legendary Chief Minister of Odisha and a freedom fighter who was himself a decorated pilot. Shri Biju Patnaik started the school on 15 January 1962 in temporary government accommodation, recognising the need for a defence-oriented residential school in eastern India.

    Within two years, under the personal care and supervision of Shri Biju Patnaik, the school’s permanent buildings and infrastructure were constructed at the current location. The new campus was inaugurated by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on 8 January 1964, a recognition of the school’s importance in India’s defence education network.

    The Bhubaneswar Setting

    The school is located in Bhubaneswar, the capital city of Odisha, known as the “Temple City of India.” The campus is situated in close proximity to Utkal University, Vani Vihar, one of the oldest universities in the state. The school is well-connected:

    • Approximately 2 km from the National Highway
    • Approximately 10 km from Biju Patnaik International Airport
    • Approximately 8 km from Bhubaneswar New Railway Station

    This central location in the state capital provides cadets with access to urban amenities while maintaining the discipline and seclusion necessary for a residential military-preparatory school.

    Role of the Sainik Schools Society

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar is managed under a three-tier system:

    1. Sainik Schools Society (Ministry of Defence) — The apex body that oversees all Sainik Schools across India
    2. Local Board of Administration (LBA) — The regional governing body comprising senior defence and civilian officials
    3. School Administration — Led by the Principal, Vice Principal, Administrative Officer, and supported by academic and administrative staff

    Academics and Curriculum

    CBSE Academic Programme

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar is permanently affiliated with the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), New Delhi. The school follows the CBSE curriculum from Class VI through Class XII, with English as the medium of instruction.

    Senior Secondary Streams:

    • Science Stream — Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology, Computer Science
    • Commerce Stream — Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics
    • Arts/Humanities — History, Geography, Political Science, Economics

    Academic Calendar

    The academic session runs from April to March, with the following assessment pattern:

    • Periodic Tests — Quarterly assessments
    • Half-Yearly Examinations — Mid-session comprehensive exams
    • Annual Examinations — End-of-year assessments following CBSE board exam pattern
    • Pre-Board Examinations — For Class X and Class XII students

    NDA Preparation

    The core mission of Sainik School Bhubaneswar is to prepare cadets for the National Defence Academy (NDA). The school provides:

    • Systematic coaching for the UPSC NDA written examination
    • Physical training aligned with NDA fitness standards
    • Personality development and leadership training
    • SSB (Services Selection Board) interview preparation
    • Guidance from experienced faculty and visiting defence officers

    Academic Results

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar consistently produces commendable results in CBSE Board Examinations. The school takes pride in the academic performance of its cadets and the number who qualify for competitive examinations including NDA, JEE, NEET, and others.

    Admission Process

    AISSEE — All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination

    Admission to Sainik School Bhubaneswar is through the All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE), conducted annually by the National Testing Agency (NTA).

    Eligibility Criteria:

    • Class VI: Boys and girls aged 10–12 years as on 31 March of the admission year
    • Class IX: Boys aged 13–15 years as on 31 March (against vacant seats)

    Reservation Policy:

    • 67% seats reserved for Odisha domicile candidates
    • 33% seats open to candidates from other states
    • Category-wise reservations: SC (15%), ST (7.5%), OBC (27%), Defence (25%)

    Application Process

    1. Online Registration — At the NTA website (exams.nta.nic.in/sainik-school-society)
    2. Entrance Examination — Conducted on the first Sunday of January each year
    3. Result and Merit List — Published by NTA
    4. Medical Examination — Shortlisted candidates undergo medical fitness assessment
    5. Document Verification and Counselling — Final admission after verification

    Fee Structure

    The annual fee at Sainik School Bhubaneswar is approximately ₹1,40,000 per year, which includes tuition, boarding, lodging, and messing charges. Fee payment can be made online through the SBI Collect portal. Scholarships are available from:

    • Ministry of Defence — For wards of defence personnel
    • Government of Odisha — State-level scholarships for SC/ST/OBC candidates
    • Merit-based Scholarships — For academically outstanding cadets

    • Sports (football, hockey, basketball, athletics, swimming)
    • Academics (quiz competitions, debate, essay writing)
    • Cultural activities (music, dance, drama)
    • Drill and parade competitions

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

    When was Sainik School Bhubaneswar established?

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar was established on 15 January 1962 by the late Shri Biju Patnaik, then Chief Minister of Odisha. The permanent campus was inaugurated by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru on 8 January 1964. It is one of the earliest Sainik Schools in India.

    Who founded Sainik School Bhubaneswar?

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar was founded by Late Shri Biju Patnaik, the Chief Minister of Odisha. Shri Biju Patnaik was a freedom fighter, decorated pilot, and visionary leader who recognised the need for a defence-oriented residential school in eastern India. He personally supervised the construction of the school’s permanent campus.

    Is Sainik School Bhubaneswar co-educational?

    Yes, Sainik School Bhubaneswar is a co-educational residential school, admitting both boys and girls. The school has separate hostel facilities for boys and girls, and provides equal opportunities for cadets of all genders in academics, sports, and NDA preparation.

    What is the fee for Sainik School Bhubaneswar?

    The annual fee at Sainik School Bhubaneswar is approximately ₹1,40,000 per year. This includes tuition, boarding, lodging, and messing charges. Scholarships are available from the Ministry of Defence, the Government of Odisha, and through merit-based schemes for eligible candidates.

    How far is Sainik School Bhubaneswar from the airport?

    Sainik School Bhubaneswar is located approximately 10 km from Biju Patnaik International Airport in Bhubaneswar. The school is also about 8 km from Bhubaneswar New Railway Station and within 2 km of the National Highway, making it easily accessible by all modes of transport.

    How can I get admission in Sainik School Bhubaneswar?

    Admission is through the All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE), conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) on the first Sunday of January each year. Candidates aged 10–12 years can apply for Class VI and those aged 13–15 for Class IX. 67% of seats are reserved for Odisha domicile candidates.
