## What Is the All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE)?

The All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE) is a nationally competitive examination conducted annually by the National Testing Agency (NTA) on behalf of the Sainik Schools Society under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India. The exam serves as the sole gateway for admission to Class 6 and Class 9 in all Sainik Schools in India.

Sainik Schools were established in 1961 with the objective of preparing students academically, physically, and mentally for entry into the National Defence Academy (NDA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), and other officer training academies. Today, there are **33 Sainik Schools** spread across every major state and union territory of India, with one new Sainik School in East Siang, Arunachal Pradesh.

The AISSEE exam tests candidates on subjects including Mathematics, General Knowledge, Language (English/Hindi), and Intelligence/Reasoning. The exam is conducted in OMR-based pen-and-paper mode, typically in the month of January each year.

## Why Choose a Sainik School?

Sainik Schools offer a unique educational experience that combines academic excellence with military-style discipline. Here are the top reasons parents choose Sainik Schools for their children:

### 1. Pathway to the Armed Forces
Sainik Schools have a proven track record of producing officers for the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force. Many alumni have gone on to serve in the National Defence Academy (NDA) and Indian Military Academy (IMA).

### 2. Holistic Development
The curriculum goes beyond academics to include:
- **Physical training** and sports (football, hockey, athletics, swimming)
- **NCC (National Cadet Corps)** training and camps
- **Leadership development** through structured activities
- **Character building** with emphasis on discipline and integrity

### 3. Affordable Education
Sainik Schools are government-aided institutions, making quality education accessible at a fraction of the cost of private boarding schools. The annual fee typically ranges from ₹50,000 to ₹1,50,000 depending on the school and category.

### 4. All-India Exposure
Students from different states and backgrounds study together, fostering national integration and cultural awareness.

### 5. Strong Alumni Network
Sainik School alumni form a powerful network of defence officers, bureaucrats, and professionals across India and the world.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. What is the All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE)?

The All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE) is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to Class 6 and Class 9 in 33+ Sainik Schools across India. The exam tests students on Mathematics, General Knowledge, Language, and Intelligence/Reasoning.

### 2. How many Sainik Schools are there in India?

As of 2026, there are **33 Sainik Schools** located across India, covering states from Tamil Nadu to Arunachal Pradesh and Gujarat to Manipur. The schools are spread across 26 states and union territories. New Sainik Schools have been added in recent years, including those in Amethi (UP), Chandrapur (Maharashtra), and Jhansi (UP).

### 3. What is the eligibility for AISSEE 2027?

For **Class 6 admission**, candidates must be aged 10–12 years as on 31 March 2027 and must be studying in Class 5. For **Class 9 admission**, candidates must be aged 13–15 years as on 31 March 2027 and must be studying in Class 8. Both categories require Indian citizenship.

### 4. What is the fee structure of Sainik Schools?

The annual fee for Sainik Schools ranges from approximately ₹50,000 to ₹1,50,000, depending on the school and the category (General, SC/ST, Defence). Fee concessions and scholarships are available for SC/ST candidates, children of defence personnel, and meritorious students.

### 5. How can I apply for AISSEE 2027?

To apply for AISSEE 2027, visit the official NTA website at [exams.nta.ac.in/AISSEE](https://exams.nta.ac.in/AISSEE) when the application window opens (expected October–November 2026). Fill in the online application form, upload required documents, pay the application fee, and submit. Keep a printout of the confirmation page for future reference.
