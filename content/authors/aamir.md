# About Aamir

Aamir has been working in defense education for over 8 years. He started as a career counselor at a coaching center in Shillong, where he noticed that most parents had no clear understanding of how Sainik Schools actually work, what the admission process involves, or what career paths open up after graduation.

That gap in accessible, honest information is what led him to Sainik School Guide India.

## Background

Aamir grew up in East Siang, Arunachal Pradesh. He attended a residential school himself, so he understands the adjustment students go through when they leave home for the first time. He studied political science at Delhi University and completed his counseling certification from the National Institute of Career Services.

His first job was at a coaching institute in Shillong, where he guided students preparing for AISSEE, RIMC, and Rashtriya Military Schools. Over 4 years, he worked with more than 500 families. Many of those students are now serving officers.

## What He Covers

**Sainik School Admissions** - Aamir writes about the admission process in plain language. He explains eligibility, documents, timelines, and common pitfalls. He keeps track of changes across all 33+ Sainik Schools.

**NDA and Defense Careers** - His articles cover the full path from Class 6 admission to NDA selection and beyond. He writes about SSB interviews, physical fitness requirements, and what life in the armed forces actually looks like.

**Parent Guidance** - A lot of his work involves helping parents understand whether Sainik School is the right fit for their child. He's honest about the challenges, not just the positives.

## Approach

Aamir writes the way he used to counsel students in person: direct, practical, and without sugarcoating. If a particular Sainik School has weak infrastructure, he says so. If the NDA selection rate is lower than what coaching centers claim, he provides the real numbers.

He sources his information from official Sainik Schools Society notifications, NTA websites, and direct conversations with school administrators and alumni.
