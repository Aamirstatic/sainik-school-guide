# About Nisha

Nisha has been writing about education for over 5 years, with a focus on entrance exams and school admissions. She joined Sainik School Guide India because she saw how confusing the AISSEE process was for parents, especially those from rural areas or first-generation school families.

Before this, she worked as a content writer for an ed-tech company in Jaipur, where she created study material for competitive exams. That experience taught her how to explain complex topics in simple language, which is what she does now for Sainik School content.

## Background

Nisha grew up in Rajasthan and completed her bachelor's in journalism from the University of Rajasthan. She started her career writing blog posts for education websites, then moved into creating detailed admission guides for various entrance exams.

Her interest in Sainik Schools began when her cousin applied for AISSEE. She saw firsthand how the family struggled to find reliable information online. Most websites either had outdated data or were trying to sell coaching courses. She decided to create content that was accurate, free, and actually helpful.

## What She Writes

**Admission Guides** - Step-by-step articles covering the entire AISSEE application process, from eligibility checks to form filling to document preparation. She updates these every year as dates and procedures change.

**Preparation Tips** - Study plans, book recommendations, daily routines, and subject-wise strategies for AISSEE. Her articles are based on what previous toppers have done, not generic advice.

**School Profiles** - Detailed information about each Sainik School, including fees, facilities, contact details, and admission statistics. She verifies data directly from school websites and official notifications.

**Student Life** - Articles about hostel life, daily routines, extracurricular activities, and what students can expect after joining a Sainik School.

## Writing Style

Nisha writes in Hinglish, mixing Hindi and English naturally, the way Indian parents actually talk. She avoids jargon and keeps sentences short. Her articles include tables, checklists, and step-by-step instructions because she knows parents are often reading these on their phones while juggling other tasks.

She fact-checks every claim against official sources. If she can't verify something, she either leaves it out or clearly marks it as unverified.
