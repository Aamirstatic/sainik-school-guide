# Sainik School Gopalganj — Bihar's Second Military School in the Land of Warriors

Bihar has a military recruitment tradition that few states can match. The Bihar Regiment, one of the Indian Army's most decorated infantry regiments, draws from the state's warrior communities — Rajputs, Bhumihars, Yadavs, and others who have served in every major conflict since independence. Sainik School Gopalganj, established on 12th October 2003 in Sipaya village near Gopalganj, was created to serve this martial heartland.

Located in the Saran division of Bihar (now part of the Gopalganj district), the school is Bihar's second Sainik School — the first being Sainik School Nalanda. The school was established as part of the Government of India's expansion of the Sainik Schools network in the early 2000s, targeting states with strong military recruitment traditions but insufficient military-oriented educational infrastructure.

## History & Legacy

### Bihar's Military Heritage

Bihar's military tradition predates modern India. The ancient kingdom of Magadha (modern Bihar) was one of the first organized states in Indian history. In the modern era, the Bihar Regiment — raised in 1941 — has fought in every major conflict: WWII, the 1947 Kashmir operations, the 1962 Sino-Indian War, the 1965 and 1971 Indo-Pak Wars, and the Kargil War. The regiment's battle cry, "Bajrang Bali Ki Jai," reflects the martial spirit of Bihar's communities.

Sainik School Gopalganj was established to channel this tradition into the officer corps. While Bihar has produced many soldiers (jawans), the Sainik School system aims to increase the state's representation in the officer cadre at the NDA.

### The Saran Division

Gopalganj is part of the Saran division, a region in northwestern Bihar along the Gandak River. The area is predominantly rural, with agriculture as the primary occupation. The Sainik School provides an alternative pathway for rural youth who might otherwise be limited to farming or low-skill employment.
