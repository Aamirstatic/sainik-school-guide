# Sainik School Kapurthala — Where French Grandeur Meets Military Discipline

There is no Sainik School in India quite like Kapurthala. While other schools were built on fresh campuses or repurposed government buildings, Sainik School Kapurthala occupies the Jagatjit Palace — a 1908 French Renaissance masterpiece modeled on the Palace of Versailles, built by Maharaja Jagatjit Singh of Kapurthala. The irony is deliberate: a palace once reserved for royalty now grooms the sons and daughters of farmers, soldiers, and civil servants for careers in the Indian Armed Forces.

Inaugurated on 8th July 1961 by Defence Minister V.K. Krishna Menon, the school was among the earliest Sainik Schools conceived under the visionary plan of Defence Minister Krishna Menon to create a feeder pipeline for the National Defence Academy. Over six decades later, the school has produced thousands of commissioned officers, including generals, admirals, and air marshals who have led India's military through conflicts and peacekeeping operations.

## History & Legacy

### The Palace That Became a School

The Jagatjit Palace was constructed in 1908 by Maharaja Jagatjit Singh Bahadur, one of the most cosmopolitan rulers of colonial India. A Francophile who spoke fluent French and maintained a villa in Nice, the Maharaja commissioned the palace in the French Renaissance style — complete with Corinthian columns, ornate cornices, and symmetrical gardens inspired by Versailles. The palace remained the seat of the Kapurthala royal family until independence.

When the Government of India decided to establish Sainik Schools in the early 1960s, the Jagatjit Palace was identified as an ideal location. The sprawling estate — measuring 1961 kanals and 18 marlas — provided ample space for academic buildings, dormitories, sports fields, and parade grounds. The transformation from royal residence to military academy school was symbolic of post-independence India's democratic ethos.

### A Legacy of Firsts

Sainik School Kapurthala was the 7th Sainik School established in India, part of the first wave of schools created between 1961 and 1963. The school's 21st Principal, Group Captain I Manoj Menon, currently leads an institution that has consistently ranked among the top Sainik Schools in NDA selections.

The school's motto — "Instilling Knowledge, Humility & Discipline" — reflects its holistic approach. Cadets are not merely coached for entrance exams; they are trained in leadership, physical fitness, and character. The piping ceremony, guard of honour, and annual athletic meets are traditions that have been maintained since the school's founding.

### The Saikap Smrithi Sthal

A unique feature of the campus is the Saikap Smrithi Sthal — a memorial dedicated to alumni who made the ultimate sacrifice in service of the nation. It serves as a daily reminder to current cadets of the weight of the uniform they aspire to wear.

## Admission Details

### Eligibility

**Class VI Admission:**
- Age: 10–12 years as on 31 March of the admission year
- Must be studying in Class V in a recognized school
- Both boys and girls eligible (girls admitted from 2021 onwards)

**Class IX Admission:**
- Age: 13–15 years as on 31 March of the admission year
- Must be studying in Class VIII in a recognized school

### Admission Process

1. **AISSEE (All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination):** Conducted by NTA (National Testing Agency) — usually held in January
2. **AISSAC Counselling:** All India Sainik Schools Admission Counselling conducted online through the NCOG portal
3. **Medical Examination:** Shortlisted candidates undergo medical fitness tests
4. **Document Verification:** Original documents verified at the school

### Domicile Reservation

As Punjab's only Sainik School, 67% of seats are reserved for Punjab domicile candidates. The remaining 33% are open to candidates from other states.
