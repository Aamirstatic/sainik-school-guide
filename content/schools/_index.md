# All Sainik Schools in India: Complete Directory 2027

**There are currently 33 Sainik Schools in India, spread across 26 states and union territories.** Each Sainik School is a residential school under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India, that follows the CBSE curriculum and offers admission to Class 6 and Class 9 through the All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE) conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA).

> **Key Takeaway:** All 33 Sainik Schools follow the same CBSE curriculum and offer admission through AISSEE. The key differences between schools are in their location, fee structure, campus facilities, and historical track record. Use this directory to compare schools and find the best fit for your child.

## Admission Process for Sainik Schools

All 33 Sainik Schools follow a centralized admission process through the All India Sainik School Entrance Exam (AISSEE):

### Step 1: Check Eligibility
- **Class 6:** Age 10–12 years as on 31 March of the admission year
- **Class 9:** Age 13–15 years as on 31 March of the admission year

### Step 2: Apply for AISSEE
- Visit [exams.nta.ac.in/AISSEE](https://exams.nta.ac.in/AISSEE) when applications open (typically October–November)
- Fill the online form and pay the application fee

### Step 3: Appear for the Exam
- AISSEE is conducted in January (pen-and-paper, OMR-based)
- Exam covers Mathematics, General Knowledge, Language, and Intelligence

### Step 4: Check Results
- Results are declared on the NTA website (typically March–April)
- Merit lists are prepared based on AISSEE scores

### Step 5: Counselling & Admission
- Qualified candidates attend counselling sessions at designated centres
- Seats are allocated based on merit, category, and school preference

## Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How many Sainik Schools are there in India in 2027?

As of 2027, there are **33 Sainik Schools** in India, spread across 26 states and union territories. The schools are located in regions from Tamil Nadu in the south to Jammu & Kashmir in the north, and from Gujarat in the west to Arunachal Pradesh in the north-east.

### 2. Which is the best Sainik School in India?

There is no single "best" Sainik School — the right choice depends on your child's needs, your location, and your priorities. Historically, schools like **Sainik School Kunjpura (Haryana)**, **Sainik School Satara (Maharashtra)**, **Sainik School Korukonda (Andhra Pradesh)**, and **Sainik School Kazhakootam (Kerala)** have had strong track records in NDA selections and academic results.

### 3. What is the fee of Sainik School per year?

The annual fee for Sainik Schools ranges from approximately **₹40,000 to ₹1,50,000** depending on the school and the student's category (General, SC/ST, Defence Ward). This is significantly lower than comparable private boarding schools. Fee concessions are available for SC/ST candidates and children of defence personnel.

### 4. Can girls apply to Sainik Schools?

Yes. The Government of India has approved the admission of girls to Sainik Schools starting from the 2021–22 academic session. Girls can apply for admission to Class 6 through AISSEE. The number of seats for girls varies by school.

### 5. Is there a Sainik School in every state?

No. While Sainik Schools cover 26 states and union territories, not every state has a Sainik School. Some states like Goa, Sikkim, Meghalaya, Tripura, Telangana, and several union territories do not have a Sainik School. However, students from any state can apply to any Sainik School through AISSEE, though a portion of seats is reserved for state domicile candidates.

### 6. What is the difference between Sainik Schools and new Sainik Schools?

Traditional Sainik Schools are fully government-funded residential schools under the Sainik Schools Society. New Sainik Schools (added from 2019 onwards) may be established in partnership with state governments, NGOs, or private entities, but they follow the same admission process through AISSEE and the same CBSE curriculum.
