# Sainik School Goalpara — Northeast India's Gateway to the Uniform

The Brahmaputra flows wide and slow through Assam, and on its southern bank, in the district town of Goalpara, sits a school that has been preparing the sons and daughters of the Northeast for military service since 1962. Sainik School Goalpara is not just another boarding school — it is the only Sainik School in Assam, and one of only two in the entire Northeast (the other being Sainik School Punglwa in Nagaland). For cadets from Assam, Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Mizoram, Tripura, and Nagaland, Goalpara is the nearest path to the National Defence Academy.

The school's 206-acre campus at Rajapara is spread across gently undulating terrain typical of lower Assam. The climate is subtropical — heavy monsoons, humid summers, and mild winters — a far cry from the dry, cold environments of many North Indian Sainik Schools. This geographical distinctiveness shapes the school's character: cadets here learn to thrive in conditions that are tropical, verdant, and sometimes unforgiving.

## History & Legacy

### Northeast's First Line of Defence

Sainik School Goalpara was established in 1962, part of the second wave of Sainik Schools created in the aftermath of the 1962 Sino-Indian War. The war had exposed vulnerabilities in India's northeastern frontier, and the establishment of a Sainik School in Assam was a strategic decision to build military awareness and officer recruitment from the region.

The school was built on 206 acres at Rajapara, in Goalpara district — a location chosen for its accessibility (on the southern bank of the Brahmaputra) and the availability of government land. The district itself has a diverse population — Assamese, Bengali, tribal communities — making the school a microcosm of the Northeast's diversity.

### The Telegraph Best School of the Year

Sainik School Goalpara received *The Telegraph* Best School of the Year award — a recognition that elevated the school's profile beyond the Sainik Schools network. The award acknowledged the school's academic performance, infrastructure development, and NDA results.

### Bir Chilarai Award

The school's NCC cadets have won the Bir Chilarai Award for Best NCC Cadet (2019-20) — named after the legendary 16th-century Assamese warrior-general Chilarai. This award connects the school's military ethos to Assam's own martial heritage.

### Admitting Girls Since 2021

Sainik School Goalpara started admitting 10 girl cadets per year from 2021, following the Government of India's directive to introduce co-education in Sainik Schools. This was a significant development for the Northeast, where educational opportunities for girls in military-oriented institutions were previously unavailable.

## Admission Details

### Eligibility

**Class VI:**
- Age: 10–12 years as on 31 March
- Currently in Class V
- Both boys and girls eligible (10 seats for girls)

**Class IX:**
- Age: 13–15 years as on 31 March
- Currently in Class VIII

### Process

1. AISSEE registration through NTA portal
2. AISSEE examination (January)
3. AISSAC online counselling — [instructions available on school website](https://sainikschoolgoalpara.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Instructions-to-Candidates-for-Online-Counselling.pdf)
4. Choice filling of schools — [guidelines](https://sainikschoolgoalpara.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/Instructions-to-Candidates-for-Choice-Filling-of-Schools.pdf)
5. Medical examination
6. Offline admission rounds for remaining vacancies

### Reservation

67% seats for Assam domicile. 33% for other states. The school also conducts offline waiting list admissions for unfilled seats.
