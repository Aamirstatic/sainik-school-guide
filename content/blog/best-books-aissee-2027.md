# Best Books for AISSEE 2027: Subject-Wise Recommendations for Class 6 & Class 9

> **Key Takeaway:** The best books for AISSEE 2027 start with NCERT textbooks as the foundation, supplemented by specialized entrance exam guides from publishers like Arihant, Upkar, and Disha. For Mathematics, use NCERT + R.D. Sharma or R.S. Aggarwal. For English, use NCERT + Wren & Martin. For GK, use Lucent's GK + Manorama Year Book. For Intelligence, use R.S. Aggarwal's Reasoning book.

Choosing the right books is one of the most important decisions in AISSEE preparation. With hundreds of books available in the market, it's easy to get overwhelmed. This guide provides expert-curated, subject-wise book recommendations for AISSEE 2027, covering both Class 6 and Class 9 aspirants.

## How to Choose the Right AISSEE Preparation Books

Before diving into recommendations, here are key criteria for selecting books:

### What Makes a Good AISSEE Preparation Book?

1. **Aligned with the latest syllabus** — Ensure the book covers the current AISSEE syllabus
2. **Based on NCERT framework** — AISSEE questions are largely NCERT-based
3. **Includes previous year papers** — Practice with actual exam questions
4. **Has detailed solutions** — Step-by-step solutions help in understanding concepts
5. **Appropriate difficulty level** — Not too easy, not beyond exam level
6. **Updated edition** — Always buy the latest edition with current year's papers

### The NCERT First Approach

**NCERT textbooks are non-negotiable** for AISSEE preparation. Here's why:
- 70–80% of AISSEE questions are directly or indirectly based on NCERT content
- NCERT provides the conceptual foundation that reference books build upon
- Many questions are verbatim from NCERT examples and exercises
- NCERT language and approach matches the exam's style

**Always complete NCERT thoroughly before moving to reference books.**

## How to Use These Books Effectively

### The 3-Layer Study Method

**Layer 1: Foundation (NCERT)**
- Read each chapter carefully
- Solve all examples and exercises
- Make notes of key concepts and formulas
- Time: 40% of total preparation time

**Layer 2: Strengthening (Reference Books)**
- Study AISSEE-specific guides for exam-oriented preparation
- Solve topic-wise practice questions
- Learn shortcuts and tricks
- Time: 35% of total preparation time

**Layer 3: Testing (Previous Year Papers & Mock Tests)**
- Solve previous year AISSEE papers
- Take mock tests under exam conditions
- Analyze mistakes and revise weak areas
- Time: 25% of total preparation time

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Are NCERT books enough for AISSEE preparation?

NCERT books are essential and form the foundation for AISSEE preparation, as 70–80% of questions are based on NCERT content. However, for comprehensive preparation, you should supplement NCERT with AISSEE-specific preparation guides (like Arihant or Upkar), reasoning books (R.S. Aggarwal), and previous year solved papers. NCERT alone may not provide sufficient practice in the Intelligence/Reasoning section, which requires specialized preparation.

### Which is the best single book for AISSEE preparation?

If you can only buy one book, the **Arihant Sainik School Entrance Exam Guide** (available separately for Class 6 and Class 9) is the best all-in-one option. It covers all subjects, includes previous year papers, has practice sets, and provides exam-oriented preparation. However, this should always be supplemented with NCERT textbooks, which are available for free.

### Should I buy the latest edition of AISSEE preparation books?

Yes, always buy the latest edition. Publishers update their books annually to include the most recent previous year papers, revised exam patterns, updated current affairs, and new practice questions. Using an older edition means you may miss recent changes in the exam pattern or syllabus. This is especially important for current affairs sections and year books.

### How many practice books should I solve for AISSEE?

Quality matters more than quantity. It's better to thoroughly solve 2–3 books than to superficially go through 10 books. Recommended approach: (1) Complete NCERT thoroughly, (2) Solve one AISSEE-specific guide (Arihant/Upkar), (3) Solve one reasoning book (R.S. Aggarwal), (4) Solve at least 5 previous year papers. This combination is sufficient for strong preparation.

### Where can I download NCERT books for free?

NCERT textbooks are available for free download in PDF format from the official NCERT website at [ncert.nic.in](https://ncert.nic.in). You can also access them through the DIKSHA app or the ePathshala app. These free digital versions are identical to the printed textbooks and are the most important study material for AISSEE preparation.
