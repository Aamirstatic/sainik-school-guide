# AISSEE 2027 Syllabus: Complete Subject-Wise Guide for Class 6 and Class 9

> **Key Takeaway:** The AISSEE 2027 syllabus for Class 6 covers Mathematics, English, General Knowledge, and Intelligence. For Class 9, it covers Mathematics, English, General Science, Social Studies, and Intelligence. The syllabus is largely based on NCERT textbooks. Mathematics carries the highest marks weightage in both classes, making it the most critical subject to prepare thoroughly.

The All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE) is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to Class 6 and Class 9 in Sainik Schools across India. Understanding the complete syllabus and exam pattern is the first step toward effective preparation. This guide provides a detailed, subject-wise breakdown of the AISSEE 2027 syllabus for both Class 6 and Class 9.

## AISSEE 2027 Exam Pattern

Before diving into the syllabus, let's understand the exam structure.

### AISSEE 2027 Exam Pattern — Class 6

| Subject | Questions | Marks per Question | Total Marks |
|---------|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Mathematics | 50 | 3 | 150 |
| English | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| General Knowledge | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| Intelligence | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| **Total** | **125** | — | **300** |

- **Duration:** 2 hours 30 minutes
- **Mode:** Offline (OMR-based)
- **Negative Marking:** No negative marking
- **Language:** Question paper in English and Hindi

### AISSEE 2027 Exam Pattern — Class 9

| Subject | Questions | Marks per Question | Total Marks |

| Mathematics | 50 | 4 | 200 |
| English | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| General Science | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| Social Studies | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| Intelligence | 25 | 2 | 50 |
| **Total** | **150** | — | **400** |

- **Duration:** 3 hours
- **Mode:** Offline (OMR-based)
- **Negative Marking:** No negative marking
- **Language:** Question paper in English and Hindi

## AISSEE 2027 Syllabus for Class 6

### Mathematics Syllabus — Class 6

The Mathematics section is based on the NCERT Class 5 syllabus with some topics from Class 6.

#### Number System
- Natural numbers and whole numbers
- Place value and face value
- Comparison and ordering of numbers
- Rounding off numbers
- Roman numerals
- Indian and International number systems

#### Arithmetic
- **Four operations:** Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division
- **Fractions:** Types (proper, improper, mixed), equivalent fractions, comparison, addition, subtraction
- **Decimals:** Place value, comparison, operations
- **HCF and LCM:** Finding by prime factorization and division method
- **Ratio and Proportion:** Simple ratios, unitary method
- **Percentage:** Conversion to/from fractions and decimals, simple percentage problems
- **Profit and Loss:** Cost price, selling price, profit/loss percentage
- **Simple Interest:** Formula and basic problems
- **Time and Distance:** Speed, distance, time calculations
- **Time and Work:** Basic problems

#### Geometry
- **Basic concepts:** Point, line, line segment, ray
- **Angles:** Types (acute, obtuse, right, straight, reflex), measurement
- **Triangles:** Types (equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right-angled), properties
- **Quadrilaterals:** Square, rectangle, parallelogram, rhombus, trapezium
- **Circles:** Radius, diameter, chord, arc, sector
- **Symmetry:** Lines of symmetry in shapes
- **3D Shapes:** Cube, cuboid, cylinder, cone, sphere — faces, edges, vertices

#### Mensuration
- Perimeter of square, rectangle, triangle
- Area of square, rectangle, triangle, circle
- Concept of volume (basic introduction)

#### Algebra (Introduction)
- Introduction to variables
- Simple equations
- Patterns and generalizations

#### Data Handling
- Pictographs
- Bar graphs
- Pie charts
- Mean, median, mode (basic)

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Is the AISSEE syllabus based on NCERT?

Yes, the AISSEE syllabus is largely based on the NCERT curriculum. For Class 6 admission, the syllabus covers topics from NCERT Class 5 (and some introductory Class 6 topics). For Class 9 admission, the syllabus is based on NCERT Class 8 content. NTA designs the AISSEE question paper keeping NCERT textbooks as the primary reference, which is why NCERT books are considered the most important study material for AISSEE preparation.

### What is the difference between AISSEE Class 6 and Class 9 syllabus?

The main differences are: (1) Class 6 has General Knowledge as a subject, while Class 9 has General Science and Social Studies as separate subjects. (2) Class 6 Mathematics carries 150 marks (50% weightage), while Class 9 Mathematics carries 200 marks (50% weightage). (3) The complexity level of questions is higher for Class 9. (4) Class 9 requires 3 hours for the exam while Class 6 requires 2.5 hours. (5) The Intelligence section difficulty increases from Class 6 to Class 9.

### Is there negative marking in AISSEE 2027?

No, there is no negative marking in AISSEE. Each correct answer earns full marks, and unanswered or wrong answers do not result in any deduction. This means students should attempt all questions, even if they need to make an educated guess. However, random guessing without any elimination is not recommended as it wastes time.

### How many questions are there in AISSEE 2027?

For Class 6, AISSEE has 125 questions carrying a total of 300 marks. For Class 9, there are 150 questions carrying a total of 400 marks. All questions are multiple-choice questions (MCQs) with four options each, and students need to mark their answers on an OMR sheet.

### What level of questions are asked in AISSEE Mathematics?

AISSEE Mathematics questions are generally of NCERT level with moderate difficulty. For Class 6, questions are based on Class 5 NCERT Mathematics with some application-based problems. For Class 9, questions are based on Class 8 NCERT Mathematics. While most questions are straightforward, 15–20% questions may require multi-step problem solving or application of concepts in unfamiliar contexts. Speed and accuracy are crucial given the time constraints.
